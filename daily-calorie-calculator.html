Daily Calorie Calculator

Our bodies require six vital nutrients in order for it to function.

Three of these nutrients are carbohydrates, fat and protein. These nutrients are considered vital as they each provide our body with a source of energy or fuel.

Energy provided by food can be measured and is called a calorie. The more active we are the more energy or fuel our bodies require. Here you will find an approximate calculation of the energy required (calories) given your activity level.

Carbohydrates

Some good sources of carbohydrates are: fruits, vegetables, whole grains and dairy products. One gram of carbohydrates is equal to four calories. Carbohydrates are best for providing energy or fuel for high intensity exercise.

Fats

Fats are also known as lipids, when in solid form lipids are called fat and in liquid form called oils. Fats provide fuel in a large concentration of calories. Fats should be consumed in moderation and are essential for many bodily functions. Some examples of good fats are: olive oil, nuts, seeds and avocados. One gram of fat provides nine calories. Fats are used by the body for sustained low intensity exercise. However, this does not mean over all body weight will be decreased.

Proteins

Proteins are not used by the body as a major energy or fuel source unless you do not consume enough carbohydrates and fats due to starvation by not eating, overtraining or by not consuming enough calories from dietary restrictions. Proteins are very complex and very important. Proteins are used by the body to build and repair muscular tissue such as muscles, tendons and ligaments. It is also very important to note good sources of protein will contain all nine essential amino acids, some examples are: fish, dairy products, meat and eggs. Incomplete protein examples are: nuts, beans, legumes and seeds. One gram of protein provides four calories.
